Gazpacho

Gazpacho is one of Spain’s most famous dishes. Made using raw blended vegetables, it’s served chilled and perfect for a light healthy refreshing summer snack, canape, lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1kg tomatoes
  • 1 small onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 sweet red peppers, grilled & peeled (can be from a tin)
  • 200g strawberries or raspberries
  • 3 tablespoons of wild rose vinegar (plus more to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 2 teabags chamomile tea

Steps

  1. Remove the core from the tomatoes, peel the onion and garlic, and put these plus all remaining ingredients, except for the chamomile tea, into a blender. Blend to a smooth, even consistency.
  2. Pour the blended soup into a container, cut open the chamomile tea bags and stir the dried chamomile into the blend. Cover the gazpacho with a lid and put in the fridge for an hour.
  3. Once infused, pour the gazpacho through a fine strainer using a spoon to help move it through a little faster (but be sure to save the pulp as it’s perfect in a tomato sauce or vegetable stew).
  4. Taste the gazpacho to see if it needs a little more salt, or a splash more acidity from the wild rose vinegar. Once seasoned, place the gazpacho in the fridge until it’s time to eat.
  5. We love to serve this with a drizzle of good olive oil, black pepper, and chili flakes. It can also be served with diced cucumber or peeled boiled shrimp as a summer main course.
